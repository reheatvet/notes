## JusTote updates submission requirements

![[Pasted image 20250521203804.png|300]]

Starting May 19th, @JusToteDAO is changing their submission requirements. Remember, they're manually reviewing all submissions since a couple of rounds back. Farmers beware. 

The new rules are:
- Grocery receipts only
- At least 3 items on the receipt
- No bag fee on the receipt
- The receipt must be no older than one week

While not required, here are two more tips that improve how quickly and easily you can be approved:
- Take the photo of the receipt with the groceries, a reusable bag or the storefront in the background
- Write down the last 4 digits from your wallet address on the receipt before submitting

Source: https://x.com/JusToteDAO/status/1923304597394706932

## The GM NFT change is implemented

The successful proposal from a few weeks back to move the GM NFT rewards from a % multiplier on voting rewards to a share based on a user's GM NFT level has now been officially implemented. 

This means that the VeB3TR pool from @veDelegate and the @VeStation\_ pool is no longer benefiting users with an exponential voting scaler, ultimately making it a lot less enticing to pool with others. 

This leads to the user's individual wallet having more of an impact thanks to the quadratic voting principle. A good thing in my opinion. 

Here's a great calculator to see what your new possible rewards are: https://vedelegate.vet/calculator/

There is concern that upgrades past the Moon tier of GM NFTs does not make much sense financially and that the GM NFT pool is not attractive enough to consider upgrading. The community is hard at work addressing these issues though so make your voice heard on Discourse. 

Here's a great proposal to start discussions with from @MrWhittleman: https://vechain.discourse.group/t/vebetterdao-proposal-restoring-the-gm-nft-system-phase-2/459?u=reheat

## veDelegate no longer receiving allocations following the approved fair use proposal

Following another proposal, this time the **fair use proposal** that was successfully passed a few weeks back, @veDelegate has left the DAO and will no longer receive any B3TR allocations. The dApp and all of its amazing features are still around, however! 

Users are able to use their services just like normal but will not receive any bonus rewards from using them anymore. It's still great if you're someone not interacting with the governance every week for example.

While the direct implications of this means that their allocations will be spread out across more dApps, its indirect implications is that ~4500 wallets might no longer be able to vote in 12 weeks as their only source of better actions for a valid vePassport has been the veDelegate rewards. 

Read more on that in my veDelegate voter report: https://notesfromthedao.com/veDelegate-Voters-Report

Source: https://x.com/veDelegate/status/1922556142376648788

## 30% base allocation gone, grant program in

The proposal to remove the 30% base allocation, changing allocations to be entirely based on users votes, was a **hot topic** in round 45-46. While it ultimately succeeded in the support & voting phase, it sparked a ton of discussion and awareness. 

dApp owners of newer dApps expressed worry that they will struggle to keep up rewards with such a large cut in allocation while at the same time the proposal is good to keep dApps undeserving of any allocation from getting 30% 'for free'. The power is now 100% in the voter's hands. 

My personal view is that it was rushed and launched at a pretty bad time with lots of moving parts, explained above. The grant program should have been defined much more clearly in advance so that we would know what exactly we and the dApps were voting on. 

Source: https://governance.vebetterdao.org/proposals/79715332253006527158658105451871709430073160636438501298296079696687567453221
